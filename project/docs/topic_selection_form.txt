SECTION B: PROJECT TITLE
================================================================================

MedBook: An Online Health Appointment Booking and Management System


================================================================================
SECTION C: BACKGROUND AND MOTIVATION
================================================================================

Access to quality healthcare remains a significant challenge across Africa, including
Kenya, where long queues, missed appointments, and poor scheduling continue to burden
both patients and healthcare providers. Many clinics and hospitals still rely on manual,
paper-based booking systems that are inefficient, prone to errors, and inaccessible to
patients outside working hours.

In developed countries such as the United Kingdom, the United States, and Australia,
digital health appointment systems have dramatically improved patient experience,
reduced waiting times, and optimized doctor availability. In Africa, countries such as
South Africa and Rwanda have begun adopting digital health platforms, yet adoption in
Kenya remains low, especially among small and mid-sized clinics.

MedBook aims to address this gap by providing a web-based system that allows patients
to book, reschedule, and cancel appointments online, while enabling healthcare
facilities to efficiently manage their schedules, patient records, and doctor
availability in real time.


================================================================================
SECTION D: PROBLEM STATEMENT
================================================================================

The current manual and fragmented appointment booking processes in Kenyan healthcare
facilities present the following problems:

1. Long physical queues at hospitals and clinics waste patients' time and reduce
   the quality of care delivered.

2. No-show appointments are common due to lack of automated reminders, leading to
   wasted doctor time and lost revenue for facilities.

3. Patients have no visibility into doctor availability before visiting a facility,
   resulting in unnecessary trips and frustration.

4. Healthcare administrators struggle to manage and retrieve patient appointment
   records efficiently due to disorganized paper-based filing systems.

SECTION E: PROJECT OBJECTIVES
================================================================================

E.1 Overall Goal
To design and develop an online health appointment booking and management system
that enables patients to book appointments remotely and healthcare facilities to
efficiently manage their schedules and patient data.

E.2 Systems Design and Development Objectives (Unique Developer Features)
i.   Doctor search and filtering by specialty, location, and availability
ii.  Automated SMS/email appointment reminders to reduce no-shows
iii. Patient medical history and appointment dashboard
iv.  Real-time appointment slot management for healthcare administrators

E.3 Specific Research Objectives
i.  To identify the traditional challenges associated with manual health appointment
    booking systems in Kenyan healthcare facilities.
ii. To determine the benefits of adopting an online health appointment booking and
    management system.

SECTION G: SCOPE OF THE PROJECT
================================================================================

MedBook will be a web-based system designed for use by patients, doctors, and
administrative staff in small to medium-sized healthcare facilities in Kenya.

Patients will be able to:
- Register and log in securely
- Search for doctors by specialty and location
- View doctor availability and book appointments
- Receive automated reminders via SMS/email
- View their appointment history and upcoming bookings

Doctors/Staff will be able to:
- Manage appointment slots and availability
- View patient booking details
- Update appointment status (confirmed, cancelled, completed)

Administrators will be able to:
- Manage system users (doctors, staff, patients)
- Generate reports on appointment statistics

The system will be implemented as a prototype targeting a healthcare facility
in Nairobi, Kenya.
