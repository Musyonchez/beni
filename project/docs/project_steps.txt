--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

WHAT TO BUILD (System):
  - Design UI mockups / wireframes for the mobile app
  - Plan: screen layouts, navigation flow, color scheme, font choices
  - Screens to design: splash, register/login, home feed, product listing,
    product detail, cart, farmer profile

WHAT TO WRITE (Report):
  CHAPTER ONE – INTRODUCTION
    1.0  Introduction (3–4 lines about FarmLink and its purpose)
    1.1  Background of the Study
           - 3 developed countries with agricultural eMarketplace systems
             (e.g. USA – FarmersWeb, UK – Farmdrop, Australia – Farmhub)
           - 2 African countries (e.g. Nigeria – Farmcrowdy, Ethiopia – ATA)
           - Kenya context: smallholder farmers lose income to middlemen,
             limited access to markets, post-harvest losses
    1.2  Problem Statement (4 problems)
           1. Farmers rely on middlemen who take large profit margins
           2. No direct digital channel between farmers and buyers
           3. Buyers cannot verify produce quality or source before purchase
           4. Post-harvest losses due to inability to find buyers quickly
    1.3  Project Objectives
           1.3.1  Overall Goal
                  "To design and develop FarmLink, an online agricultural
                  produce eMarketplace for Kenyan smallholder farmers."
           1.3.2  Systems Design Objectives (4 unique developer features)
                  i.   Geolocation – buyers discover nearby farmers and produce
                  ii.  Shopping cart with M-Pesa payment integration
                  iii. Real-time order tracking from farm to buyer
                  iv.  Seller rating and review system for quality assurance
           1.3.3  Specific Research Objectives
                  SRO1: To identify challenges faced by smallholder farmers
                        using traditional/manual market systems in Kenya
                  SRO2: To determine the benefits of an online agricultural
                        produce eMarketplace for Kenyan smallholder farmers
    1.4  Project Questions
    1.5  Scope (farmer/buyer/admin roles, Nairobi + peri-urban areas, prototype)
    1.6  Limitations (14-week timeline, internet access in rural areas, budget)
    1.7  Significance
           - SDG 2: Zero Hunger (food security, sustainable agriculture)
           - SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth (fair income for farmers)
           - AU Agenda 2063 Aspiration 1: Prosperous Africa
