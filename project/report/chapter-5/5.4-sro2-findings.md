# 5.4 Findings on SRO2: Benefits of an Online Agricultural eMarketplace

Findings in this section draw from both farmer and buyer respondents, as the value of the platform depends on adoption and engagement from both sides of the market.

## 5.4.3 Most Desired Features

## 5.4.4 Perceived Benefit: Income Improvement

When farmer respondents were asked whether they believed selling directly to buyers through a mobile platform would improve their income, [X]% agreed or strongly agreed. Responses from informal interviews supported this view — one respondent noted that the ability to set prices based on current demand, rather than accepting whatever a broker offered, would represent a meaningful improvement in their household income. This perception aligns with the findings of Aker and Mbiti (2010), who demonstrated that mobile-enabled market access improves income outcomes for agricultural producers by reducing information asymmetry and enabling direct price negotiation.

## 5.4.5 Summary of SRO2 Findings

The findings confirm that both farmers and buyers in the study area perceive clear benefits from the adoption of a mobile agricultural eMarketplace. Expressed willingness to use such a platform is high among both groups, M-Pesa is already the dominant payment method, and the four core features of FarmLink — geolocation, integrated payment, order tracking, and seller ratings — were independently identified by respondents as the most valued capabilities. These findings collectively validate the design choices made in Chapter Four and provide empirical support for the development of FarmLink as a solution to the challenges identified in SRO1.
