# 4.3 Stakeholders

The following stakeholders were identified as having a direct interest in or interaction with the FarmLink system:

**1. Smallholder Farmers (Primary Users — Sellers)**
Farmers are the primary supply-side users of FarmLink. They interact with the system to create and manage produce listings, receive and confirm orders from buyers, update order status as produce moves from farm to delivery, and view their earnings and buyer ratings. Farmers benefit from the system by gaining direct market access, eliminating intermediaries, and building a digital reputation through the rating system.

**2. Buyers / Consumers (Primary Users — Buyers)**
Buyers are the primary demand-side users of FarmLink. They use the system to discover nearby farmers, browse and purchase fresh produce, complete payments via M-Pesa, track their orders, and leave reviews. Buyers benefit from access to fresher, locally sourced produce at competitive prices and the convenience of mobile purchasing.

**3. System Administrator**
The administrator manages the FarmLink platform from a privileged account. Administrative responsibilities include verifying farmer accounts, moderating produce listings, managing user access, and generating platform-wide reports. The administrator does not buy or sell produce but ensures the integrity and quality of the marketplace.

**4. Safaricom (M-Pesa / Daraja API)**
Safaricom is an external stakeholder providing the payment infrastructure through the M-Pesa Daraja API. The system depends on Safaricom's STK Push service to initiate mobile payments and on the Daraja callback mechanism to confirm transaction outcomes. Safaricom's API terms and sandbox environment constrain the payment implementation during the prototype phase.

**5. Twilio**
Twilio is an external stakeholder providing SMS notification infrastructure. The system uses Twilio's SMS API to deliver order status alerts to farmers and buyers via text message, complementing the in-app push notification system.

**6. Developer (Beni Ishimwe)**
The developer is responsible for designing, building, testing, and documenting the FarmLink system. During the prototype phase, the developer also serves as the de facto system administrator.
