# 1.0 Introduction

FarmLink is a mobile-based agricultural produce eMarketplace designed to connect Kenyan smallholder farmers directly with buyers, eliminating the reliance on intermediaries that have long suppressed farmer incomes. Agriculture remains the backbone of Kenya's economy, contributing approximately 33% of the country's Gross Domestic Product (GDP) and employing over 40% of the total population (Kenya National Bureau of Statistics, 2022). Despite this significance, the majority of smallholder farmers continue to operate at subsistence level, largely due to limited access to fair and direct markets, resulting in low income and substantial post-harvest losses. FarmLink addresses this gap by providing a platform through which farmers can list their produce, buyers can discover and purchase directly, and transactions are completed securely via M-Pesa — Kenya's dominant mobile money platform. This chapter presents the background of the study, the problem statement, project objectives, project questions, scope, limitations, and the significance of the project.
