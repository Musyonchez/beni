# 1.5 Scope of the Project

FarmLink will be developed as a cross-platform mobile application built using React Native, with a Node.js backend and MongoDB database. The system is designed for use by three categories of users — farmers, buyers, and administrators — operating within small to medium agricultural supply chains in Kenya. The prototype will target smallholder farmers in the peri-urban areas of Nairobi County, including Limuru, Kikuyu, and Kiambu, with buyers primarily located in Nairobi City.

**Farmer (Seller) functionality:**
Farmers will be able to register on the platform and create a profile that includes their name, location, and a short bio. They will be able to list produce for sale by specifying the product name, category, price per unit, available quantity, unit of measurement, and photographs. Farmers will receive notifications when buyers place orders, and will be able to confirm or reject orders and mark them as dispatched. Farmers will also have access to a dashboard showing their earnings history and buyer ratings received.

**Buyer (Consumer) functionality:**
Buyers will be able to register, browse available produce by category or search by keyword, and filter results based on their current geolocation to find nearby farmers. Buyers will be able to view detailed farmer profiles including ratings and reviews, add items to a shopping cart, and complete purchases using M-Pesa. Once an order is placed, buyers will be able to track its status in real time and, upon delivery, submit a rating and review for the farmer.

**Administrator functionality:**
Administrators will be able to manage all registered users, moderate produce listings to ensure compliance with platform standards, view system-wide reports on sales volumes, active users, and popular product categories, and deactivate accounts where necessary.

**Stakeholders:**
The primary stakeholders of the FarmLink system are smallholder farmers, buyers and consumers, the system administrator, Safaricom as the M-Pesa payment gateway provider, and the developer, Beni Ishimwe.

**Out of scope:**
The following functionalities are explicitly excluded from this version of the system: physical delivery and logistics coordination, integration with the National Hospital Insurance Fund (NHIF) or any government agricultural subsidy systems, bulk or wholesale B2B transactions targeting restaurants or supermarkets, and multi-currency or international payment processing.
