# 1.7 Significance of the Study

The development of FarmLink carries significance at multiple levels — from the individual farmer household to the broader national and continental development agenda. This section outlines the project's contribution to sustainable development, economic growth, and digital transformation in Kenya and Africa.

**Contribution to SDG 2 — Zero Hunger**
This project is directly relevant to the United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 2, which aims to end hunger, achieve food security, improve nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture by the year 2030. Specifically, FarmLink supports SDG 2 Target 2.3, which calls for doubling the agricultural productivity and income of small-scale food producers, including through secure and equal access to markets (United Nations, 2015). By enabling farmers to sell directly to buyers at fair prices, the platform reduces the income lost to intermediaries and incentivises increased agricultural production. Furthermore, by connecting buyers to fresh local produce more efficiently, FarmLink contributes to improved food availability and reduced post-harvest waste — both of which are central to food security.

**Contribution to SDG 8 — Decent Work and Economic Growth**
FarmLink also contributes to SDG 8, which promotes sustained, inclusive, and sustainable economic growth, full and productive employment, and decent work for all. The platform creates economic opportunity for smallholder farmers — one of Kenya's most economically vulnerable groups — by restoring their agency in the marketplace. Rather than accepting prices dictated by middlemen, farmers using FarmLink are empowered to set their own prices, access a broader buyer pool, and build a digital transaction record that can support future access to credit and financial services.

**Contribution to AU Agenda 2063 — Aspiration 1**
At the continental level, FarmLink aligns with the African Union's Agenda 2063, specifically Aspiration 1, which envisions "a prosperous Africa based on inclusive growth and sustainable development." The Agenda 2063 framework identifies digital transformation of the agricultural sector as a key driver of rural economic development and improved livelihoods across the continent (African Union, 2015). By demonstrating a viable, mobile-first model for agricultural eCommerce in the Kenyan context, FarmLink contributes to the broader vision of a digitally enabled African economy in which smallholder farmers are active and empowered participants.

**Local Impact in Kenya**
Within Kenya specifically, FarmLink has the potential to meaningfully reduce the income gap between smallholder farmers and urban consumers by eliminating unnecessary intermediary layers from the agricultural supply chain. The platform's geolocation feature reduces buyer travel time and delivery distance, making locally grown produce more competitive with supermarket alternatives. Over time, the accumulation of transaction data within the platform can provide both individual farmers and agricultural policymakers with valuable insights into demand patterns, seasonal trends, and pricing benchmarks — information that has historically been inaccessible to smallholder farmers operating outside formal market structures.
