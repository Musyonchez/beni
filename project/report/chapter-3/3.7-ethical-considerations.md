# 3.7 Ethical Clearance Considerations

The following ethical principles were observed throughout the research and development process:

**Informed Consent**
All questionnaire respondents and interview participants were informed of the purpose of the study before participating. Participation was entirely voluntary, and respondents were made aware that they could withdraw at any time without consequence. A brief consent statement was included at the top of the questionnaire, which respondents acknowledged before proceeding.

**Anonymity and Confidentiality**
No personal identifying information — including names, phone numbers, or addresses — was collected through the questionnaire. Responses were recorded anonymously to protect the privacy of all respondents. Any identifying information shared during informal interviews was treated as confidential and was not attributed to specific individuals in this report.

**Data Use and Storage**
All primary data collected was used solely for the academic purposes of this project. Questionnaire responses were stored securely on a password-protected device and will be deleted upon completion of the project. No data was shared with third parties.

**Honesty and Academic Integrity**
The system developed as part of this project is the original work of the researcher. All external sources consulted in the preparation of this report have been properly cited in accordance with APA 7th edition guidelines. The researcher declares that no portion of this work has been submitted for academic credit in any other course or institution.

**Do No Harm**
The research process did not expose participants to any physical, psychological, or financial risk. Questions in the questionnaire were non-sensitive in nature and did not solicit information that could cause embarrassment or harm to respondents.
