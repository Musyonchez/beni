# 3.1 Locality of the Project and Beneficiaries

FarmLink is designed for implementation in Kenya, with the prototype targeting smallholder farmers operating in the peri-urban areas of Nairobi County, specifically in the agricultural zones of Limuru, Kikuyu, and Kiambu. These areas were selected due to their proximity to Nairobi City — Kenya's primary urban consumer market — and their high concentration of smallholder horticultural farmers who produce vegetables, fruits, and other perishable produce for urban consumption. The short distance between these farming zones and the city centre makes geolocation-based direct sales both logistically viable and commercially attractive for the target user base.

The primary beneficiaries of the FarmLink system are:

**Smallholder farmers (sellers):** Farmers in peri-urban Nairobi who currently rely on brokers or physical markets to sell their produce and who stand to benefit from direct market access, fair pricing, and digital transaction records.

**Buyers (consumers):** Urban residents in Nairobi seeking fresh, locally sourced produce at competitive prices, who would benefit from the convenience of discovering nearby farmers and purchasing online via M-Pesa.

**Healthcare administrators and system admin:** The system administrator responsible for managing platform users, moderating listings, and generating operational reports.

Secondary beneficiaries include agricultural policymakers and development organisations who may use the platform's transaction data to understand smallholder market dynamics and inform policy decisions related to food security and rural income.
