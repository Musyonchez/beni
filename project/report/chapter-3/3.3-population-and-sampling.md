# 3.3 Population of the Study and Sampling Method

## Target Population

The target population of this study comprised two groups: smallholder farmers in the peri-urban areas of Limuru, Kikuyu, and Kiambu in Nairobi County, and buyers residing in Nairobi City who purchase fresh produce for household consumption. Both groups represent the direct end-users of the FarmLink system and were therefore the most relevant sources of primary data for the study's research objectives.

## Sample Size

Given the time and resource constraints of a 14-week student project, a sample of 40 respondents was targeted — 20 farmers and 20 buyers. This sample size, while modest, is considered sufficient for a descriptive study of this scope, particularly when combined with purposive sampling to ensure respondents are highly relevant to the research questions (Kombo & Tromp, 2006).

## Sampling Method

**Purposive sampling** was adopted for this study. Purposive sampling is a non-probability sampling technique in which respondents are selected deliberately based on their relevance to the research objectives, rather than at random (Creswell, 2014). This approach was appropriate for this study because both the farmer and buyer samples needed to meet specific criteria to provide meaningful responses:

- **Farmers** were required to be active smallholder farmers selling produce and to have at least some prior experience using a mobile phone for communication or financial transactions.
- **Buyers** were required to be urban residents who regularly purchase fresh produce and own a smartphone with mobile internet access.

Farmer respondents were accessed through local farmer cooperatives, Savings and Credit Cooperative Organisations (SACCOs), and contacts at Wakulima Market in Nairobi. Buyer respondents were recruited from residential areas of Nairobi through personal networks and community groups.
