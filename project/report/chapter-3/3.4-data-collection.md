# 3.4 Data Collection Methods

## Primary Data Collection

Primary data was collected directly from the target population using the following methods:

**Structured Questionnaire**
The primary instrument for data collection was a structured questionnaire administered to both farmer and buyer respondents (see Appendix 9.1). The questionnaire was divided into sections corresponding to the study's research objectives. Section A captured respondent demographic data including gender, education level, age range, and farming experience (for farmers) or frequency of produce purchasing (for buyers). Section B contained questions targeting Specific Research Objective 1 — the challenges of traditional market systems — and Section C contained questions targeting Specific Research Objective 2 — the perceived benefits of an online eMarketplace. Questions used a combination of Likert scale responses (Strongly Agree to Strongly Disagree), multiple choice, and short open-ended items.

The questionnaire was designed in both English and Swahili to maximise accessibility for farmer respondents with varying levels of English literacy. It was administered in person at farmer cooperative meetings and community locations to ensure a high response rate and to allow the researcher to clarify any questions that respondents found unclear.

**Informal Interviews**
To supplement the questionnaire data, brief informal interviews were conducted with a subset of five farmers and three buyers. These interviews were unstructured and conversational in nature, focused on understanding the day-to-day experience of selling or buying produce and exploring any concerns about adopting a digital platform. Notes from these interviews were used to inform the system design decisions documented in Chapter Four, particularly the user roles, functional requirements, and scope boundaries.

## Secondary Data Collection

Secondary data was gathered from published sources including academic journals, government reports, international organisation publications (FAO, World Bank, GSMA), and reputable online platforms. These sources informed the background, literature review, and significance sections of the report and are fully cited in the References section.
