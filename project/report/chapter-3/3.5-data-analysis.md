# 3.5 Data Analysis Methods

Quantitative data collected from the structured questionnaire was analysed using descriptive statistics. Responses were tallied, converted into percentages, and presented in the form of frequency tables and bar charts in Chapter Five. This approach enabled clear visual communication of the distribution of respondent opinions and experiences in relation to each research objective.

For Likert scale items, responses were aggregated to show the proportion of respondents who agreed (Agree + Strongly Agree), were neutral, or disagreed (Disagree + Strongly Disagree) with each statement. Percentage values were used to draw conclusions and identify patterns in the data.

Qualitative data from informal interviews was analysed thematically. Common themes and recurring concerns identified across multiple interviewees were noted and used to enrich the interpretation of the quantitative findings in Chapter Five and the recommendations in Chapter Six. No formal coding software was used; themes were identified manually through repeated review of interview notes.

All data analysis was conducted using Microsoft Excel for tabulation and chart generation. Charts produced in Excel were exported and embedded in the relevant sections of the report.
