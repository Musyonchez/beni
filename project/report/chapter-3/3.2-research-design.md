# 3.2 Research Design Approach

This study adopted a descriptive research design. A descriptive design was selected because the primary aim of the research component of this project is to describe and document the current state of agricultural market access among smallholder farmers in Kenya, and to capture the perceptions and needs of the target users with respect to a digital eMarketplace solution. Descriptive research is appropriate when the objective is to accurately characterise a phenomenon as it exists, without manipulating variables or testing causal relationships (Creswell, 2014).

Within the descriptive framework, this study employed a mixed-methods approach, combining quantitative data gathered through a structured questionnaire with qualitative insights collected through informal interviews with farmers and buyers. The quantitative data provided measurable evidence of the challenges faced by farmers using traditional market systems and the level of openness among buyers to adopting an online purchasing platform. The qualitative insights complemented the numerical findings by capturing the lived experiences, concerns, and suggestions of the target users in their own words — context that is essential for designing a system that is not only technically functional but also practically usable by its intended audience.
