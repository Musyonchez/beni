# 6.3 Recommendations

Based on the findings and conclusions of this study, the following recommendations are made to three categories of stakeholders.

## 6.3.1 Recommendations for Smallholder Farmers

Smallholder farmers in the peri-urban regions of Kiambu County are encouraged to register on the FarmLink platform and complete their profiles with accurate location data, produce photographs, and current pricing to maximise discoverability by nearby buyers. Farmers who operate within cooperatives or SACCOs are further encouraged to coordinate listings across group members, as produce aggregated at a cooperative level is more attractive to buyers requiring larger quantities and creates a stronger collective presence on the platform. Farmers should also take advantage of the real-time order notification feature to harvest produce only once a confirmed buyer order has been placed, directly addressing the post-harvest loss challenge identified in the research findings.

## 6.3.2 Recommendations for Government and Regulatory Bodies

The national and county governments are encouraged to invest in rural internet infrastructure, particularly in peri-urban farming areas where mobile data access remains expensive relative to farmer income levels. Subsidised data bundles for agricultural applications — similar to the zero-rating of agricultural content trialled in other East African markets — would significantly reduce the connectivity barrier identified in the limitations of this study. Furthermore, the government should consider formally recognising and supporting community-level digital marketplace initiatives as part of the national Big Four Agenda pillar on food security and the Kenya Digital Economy Blueprint, as platforms such as FarmLink directly contribute to the agricultural market access targets therein.

## 6.3.3 Recommendations for Future Developers

Developers building upon or extending the FarmLink system are encouraged to integrate a logistics coordination module that connects farmers and buyers with third-party motorcycle courier networks (such as boda boda operators) for last-mile delivery, a feature that was explicitly excluded from the current scope but frequently mentioned by respondents as a desired capability. Additionally, future developers should consider implementing a cooperative group account model that allows multiple farmers within a SACCO to manage a shared storefront, pool produce listings, and distribute earnings digitally — a feature that would make the platform viable for organised farmer groups and could significantly increase adoption in rural areas.
