# 6.2 Conclusion

This study set out to address two specific research objectives through a combination of primary research and the design and development of the FarmLink system. The first objective — to identify the challenges faced by smallholder farmers when using traditional and intermediary-based market systems in Kenya — has been addressed through empirical evidence gathered from farmer respondents in Limuru, Kikuyu, and Kiambu. The findings confirm that the majority of farmers in the study area depend on middlemen for market access, receive prices that do not reflect the true market value of their produce, and experience preventable post-harvest losses due to the absence of a direct and timely buyer discovery mechanism.

The second objective — to determine the perceived benefits of adopting an online agricultural produce eMarketplace for Kenyan smallholder farmers — has been addressed through data gathered from both farmer and buyer respondents. The findings confirm that willingness to adopt a mobile-first platform is high among both groups, that M-Pesa is already the dominant payment method in the target population, and that the specific features implemented in FarmLink — geolocation discovery, integrated payment, real-time order tracking, and seller ratings — are those most valued by potential users.

In response to the first research question, the traditional market systems currently used by smallholder farmers in the study area are characterised by intermediary exploitation, information asymmetry, and limited geographic market reach, all of which suppress farmer income and contribute to post-harvest losses. In response to the second research question, an online agricultural produce eMarketplace offers measurable benefits including direct buyer access, price transparency, reduced post-harvest losses through faster buyer discovery, and mobile payment security through M-Pesa integration. These conclusions confirm that the development of FarmLink is both empirically justified and practically viable within the Kenyan market context.
