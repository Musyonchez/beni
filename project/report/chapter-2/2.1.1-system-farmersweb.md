# 2.1.1 System One: FarmersWeb — United States of America

**System Name:** FarmersWeb
**Country:** United States of America
**URL:** https://www.farmersweb.com

FarmersWeb is a B2B (business-to-business) online agricultural marketplace founded in 2013 and headquartered in New York, USA. The platform was designed to streamline the process by which local farms sell fresh produce to institutional buyers such as restaurants, hotels, corporate caterers, and specialty grocery stores. Rather than routing transactions through traditional food distributors, FarmersWeb enables farms to list their available inventory in real time, set their own pricing, and receive orders directly from buyers within their delivery radius. The system includes features such as digital invoicing, order management, inventory tracking, and delivery scheduling — all accessible through a web-based interface (FarmersWeb, 2022).

**Strengths:** FarmersWeb offers a clean and intuitive interface that simplifies the ordering process for both farms and institutional buyers. The platform empowers farmers to set their own prices and maintain control over their supply, which results in higher profit margins compared to traditional distributor relationships. It also provides farms with digital transaction records, which support accounting and financial planning.

**Weaknesses:** FarmersWeb is exclusively designed for the B2B market, meaning individual consumers cannot purchase directly from farms through the platform. It is also limited to the United States market and requires farmers to have reliable internet access and a degree of digital literacy to operate effectively. The subscription-based pricing model may be a barrier for smallholder farmers with limited financial resources. Furthermore, the platform does not incorporate geolocation or mobile payment features, limiting its accessibility in low-income or mobile-first markets such as Kenya.
