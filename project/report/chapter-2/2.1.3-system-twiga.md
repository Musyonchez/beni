# 2.1.3 System Three: Twiga Foods — Kenya

**System Name:** Twiga Foods
**Country:** Kenya
**URL:** https://www.twiga.com

Twiga Foods is a Kenyan B2B agricultural supply chain platform founded in 2014 that sources fresh produce directly from smallholder farmers and distributes it to urban food vendors, kiosks, and small retailers across Nairobi and other Kenyan cities. The platform uses a mobile-based ordering system that allows vendors to place orders via a mobile application, and operates a fleet of refrigerated vehicles to collect produce from farmers and deliver to buyers. Twiga Foods has onboarded over 17,000 farmers and serves more than 8,000 vendors across Kenya, making it one of the largest agri-tech platforms on the continent (Twiga Foods, 2023). The platform also offers farmers access to inputs such as fertiliser and seeds through a digital credit system linked to their transaction history on the platform.

**Strengths:** Twiga Foods is the most contextually relevant system reviewed in this study, as it operates within the same Kenyan agricultural ecosystem that FarmLink targets. The platform's mobile-first approach, integration with the local supply chain, and use of digital transaction records to extend credit to farmers demonstrate a deep understanding of the needs and constraints of Kenyan smallholder farmers. Twiga's scale also validates the commercial viability of technology-driven agricultural marketplaces in the Kenyan market.

**Weaknesses:** Despite its scale, Twiga Foods operates exclusively as a B2B platform, meaning individual consumers cannot purchase directly from farmers through the system. The pricing model is determined by Twiga rather than by farmers, which limits farmer autonomy and may still result in prices below what the open market would yield in a direct-sale scenario. Additionally, the platform focuses on logistics management rather than marketplace discovery — buyers cannot browse farmer profiles, check ratings, or select specific farms. These gaps represent the core value proposition of FarmLink, which is designed to give individual consumers direct access to farmer produce with full transparency, geolocation-based discovery, and a peer-review system.
